GENERAL


What is special about Judge it!?

Judge it! is a voting app that facilitates quick decisions with several people, fun votings or instant feedback from many people on any topic. We are thus clearly no common messenger app, where rapid, coordinated decisions amoung groups of people are really hard to come by. Special about Judge it! is that you have everything combined in one app: you can quickly create votings, vote, follow the results in real time and chat directly with the voting participants when needed on a separate screen. The app notifies you about the activities of the other participants so that the organization is easy and fun. Also, you can chat with your contacts without voting at all, or vote with the Judge it! community anonymously (public voting).


In which languages is ​​Judge it! available?

Judge it! is available in English, German, Spanish, French and Portuguese. In the future, we plan to launch even more languages.


On which devices can I use Judge it!?

Judge it! can be used on all relevant iOS and Android devices. A desktop version is not currently being considered, as our surveys have shown that interaction and instant notifications are the most important thing for you when it comes to using our app.



FEATURES AND SCREENS - CONTACTS


Why are no friends of mine shown in the "contacts" tab?

Friends will only be displayed in your "contacts" screen if as part of the registration process they correctly entered their cell phone number in Judge it! and if that number is also stored in the address book of your phone. In addition, you must have the feature enabled that Judge it! has access to these contacts. Moreover, you can use the search function to look for nicknames of your friends and then add them to your Judge it! contacts.


Can I create groups in Judge it!?

Yes. In the contacts screen there is an own icon for the creation of a group. The creation of a group makes it possible that not all individual contacts must be selected each and every time when often creating votings with the same people. For example, you can create groups for groups of friends, your sports club, a poker group or your family. Of course you can subsequently change the members of a group. When creating a group, the group will appear in the contacts section of all group members.



FEATURES AND SCREENS – CREATE NEW VOTING


How can I create a new voting?

To create a new voting, you simply have to click on the question mark in the top right corner from within the private or public main screen. You will then directly see the first screen of a new voting. First you have to enter a question or topic, below that you can enter several voting options. Please notice that the logic for all participants taking part in a voting is always the same: for each voting option, participants can vote positively and negatively (green and red Smileys).



What kind of votings can I create?

With Judge it! you can vote with an unlimited number of friends about dates, photos, text and link options - and this even in a multidimensional way; i.e., even within a one voting you can vote about a place AND a time for a reunion with your friends at the same time. For every voting straight-forward real-time results will be shown.


Who can I invite to a voting?

As voting participants you can choose between the following options on the second screen of a new voting:

  1. Invite the Judge it! community (you can ask users using the app in the same language as you on their opinion on certain topics). In order to use this function you do not have to enable location based services for the app. This voting is public and anonymous and you can find all public votings on the public screen that is accessible from the main screen.


  1. Invite friends via link (broadcast). You can share this link via messengers, send it via email, or post it on social networks.


  1. You can invite individual friends (if in your contacts list) and select groups you created or you were invited to.



FEATURES AND SCREENS – WITHIN A VOTING


What screens can I see within a voting?

Each voting consists of 3 screens: the voting screen, the results screen, and the chat screen.

In the voting screen you can vote positively or negatively on each voting option by using the two buttons.

The results screen shows the voting results per voting option in pie chart format consolidated for all participants. Here you can see how many participants voted positively and negatively for each voting option, and what the approval rate is (i.e.: 3 people say yes, they want to go to London, 7 say no, the approval rate is 30%).


Below that you can see a detailed table showing you who voted how on which option (positive or negative, i.e. agreement or disagreement).


And last but not least, you can see at the bottom which participants are invited to that voting and who has already voted and who hasn’t, or eventually who has left the voting.


Within a voting, who can see the chat?

Only the participants of that specific voting. You can see the participants at the bottom of the results screen as outlined above. Please notice that for link (broadcast) and public votings, new participants can at any time enter the voting and see the chat and results.


Can I subsequently add participants or voting options later on?

Yes; there is a button in the upper right corner on the 3 activity screens within each voting. Please notice the following details, however:


New participants

When it comes to private votings (contacts / groups were individually selected), only the creator of a voting can add voting participants. By this, we ensure that with private votings no „unwanted“ participants will show up. If instead you created a link (broadcast) or a public (community) voting, at any time new participants can show up.


New voting options

With private votings (contacts / groups were individually selected), all participants can subsequently add voting options. This facilitates a brainstorming among participants before actually voting in order to jointly come up with voting options to vote upon. At the same time, this prevents everything from having to be discussed in a chaotic manner via the chat function. With link (broadcast) and public votings, only the creator of the voting can add voting options.


What is the purpose of the function “Close voting”?

Within a voting, behind the button in the top right corner, the creator of the voting can select the function “close voting”. Clicking this button triggers that participants cannot vote anymore. This will be communicated in the voting specific chat.



FEATURES AND SCREENS - CHAT


Can I also just chat without creating a voting?

Yes, you can. On the main screen you can directly access the chatscreen for private chats. You can create a new chat with single persons or a group by clicking on the pencil icon in the top bar on the right hand side.
Within a chat you can create a voting with the chat participants by clicking on the question mark symbol in the top right corner. The chat participants will automatically be selected for purposes of that voting. The voting will appear on the private voting screen.


SECURITY AND PRIVACY


Is my personal information safe?

Ever since we had the idea to create Judge it!, data security and privacy were a top priority and thus very important to us. We would like to refer to our detailed explanations in the Privacy Policy, which can be found in the settings tab in the sidebar. We are of course happy to take your questions on top of that. If you want to get in touch with us, just write to info@judge-it.net.


What is the purpose of the function “block user”?

When swiping to the left on a voting or clicking on a miniature picture of a user, you can block the creator of a voting / the user. This makes sense when you do not want to receive votings or chat comments from a certain user anymore. Upon blocking, the user will appear as “blocked” in your contacts list. Of course, you can at any time unblock a user again. Also, there is a privacy mode that you can toggle on and off from within the settings.



OTHER TOPICS


I am getting annoyed of the notifications. What can I do?

You can deactivate all notifications for the whole app from within the settings. If you would like to deactivate the notifications of just one voting, you can deactivate the notifications of that voting only by swiping to the left on a voting on the main screen and selecting the function “deactivate voting”. For public votings, notifications are deactivated by default.


Can I leave a voting? What is the consequence?

Yes, you can leave a voting on the main screen of the app by swiping to the left on a voting and selecting “delete voting”. Here, you will find even more functions (deactivate notifications, block user, highlight voting). The consequence of leaving a voting is that this voting will not show up on the home screen of the app anymore and that going forward you will not receive any further notifications. The other participants will be notified in the chat that you left the voting.



I just got a new phone number – what to do now?

That is no problem at all. Regardless of your phone number you can login with Judge it! with a new phone or phone number. Please notice that friends that have not yet stored your new phone number under their phone contacts and that want to find you might not be able to find you within the app.
